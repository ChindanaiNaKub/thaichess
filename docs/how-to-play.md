# How to Play ThaiChess

ThaiChess (หมากรุก) is the traditional chess of Thailand, closely related to the ancient Indian game Chaturanga. This guide covers the rules and piece movements.

## The Board & Pieces

ThaiChess is played on an 8×8 board. Each player starts with 16 pieces:

| Piece | Thai Name | Count | Movement |
|-------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Khun (King) | ขุน | 1 | 1 square in any direction |
| Met (Queen) | เม็ด | 1 | 1 square diagonally |
| Khon (Bishop) | โคน | 2 | 1 square diagonally or 1 forward |
| Rua (Rook) | เรือ | 2 | Any distance horizontally/vertically |
| Ma (Knight) | ม้า | 2 | L-shape: 2 squares in one direction, 1 in perpendicular |
| Bia (Pawn) | เบี้ย | 8 | 1 square forward; captures 1 square diagonally forward |

## Key Differences from Western Chess

### Pawns
- Start on the **3rd rank** (not 2nd)
- Promote on the **6th rank** (not 8th)
- No double-step first move
- No en passant

### Queen
- Moves **only 1 square diagonally** (much weaker than Western chess queen)
- Cannot move in straight lines

### Bishop
- Moves **1 square diagonally OR 1 square forward**
- More flexible than Western bishop

### No Castling
- The special castling move does not exist in ThaiChess

### Special Rules
- **Check & Checkmate** — Same as Western chess
- **Draws** — Stalemate, threefold repetition, and 50-move rule

## Winning

- **Checkmate** — Opponent's king is in check and has no legal moves
- **Resignation** — Opponent gives up
- **Time** — Opponent's time runs out (in timed games)
- **Counting** — In some variants, the player with more material wins after a certain phase

## Strategy Tips

1. **Control the center** — The 4th and 5th ranks are crucial
2. **Protect your king** — Develop pieces to defend
3. **Develop early** — Get knights and bishops out before moving the queen
4. **Pawns are weak** — Protect them and advance carefully
5. **Use the queen sparingly** — Your queen can only move 1 square, so position matters
